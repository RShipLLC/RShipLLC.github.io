Why Here?

This building is located in the heart of South James Street; the corner of South James and Foster, in the City of Ludington. The original build is around 1890 with a 20x50 rear addition built in approximately 1910. A mural of Father Marquette, landing on the shores around Ludington encompasses its south side. There are three commercial units; two store fronts are on South James; the third unit is on Foster. Since our purchase, the two store fronts on South James have been restored. The space reveals its character with original tin ceilings and maple floor yet, has the upgrade of efficient heating and cooling systems.

Being in the heart of South James Street brings significant pedestrian traffic to the space. South James Street is home to various restaurants, breweries and retail; the location of the building is within walking distance of the event center, parks, marinaâ€™s, waterfront and all of downtown.
